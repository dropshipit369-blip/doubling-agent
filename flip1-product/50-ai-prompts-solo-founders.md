## 1. Business Strategy (10 prompts)

### 1.1 Idea Validation
```
I want to build [product idea] for [target audience]. Before I write a line of code, poke holes in this idea. Tell me: (1) who specifically would pay for this and why, (2) what they're currently using instead, (3) the 3 biggest reasons this fails, (4) one pivot that makes it stronger. Be brutally honest.
```

### 1.2 Competitive Landscape
```
I'm building [product type] in the [industry] space. Find me the top 5 competitors, what they charge, what customers complain about in their reviews, and where the gap is that I can exploit as a solo founder with no funding. Focus on weaknesses I can actually capitalize on, not theoretical advantages.
```

### 1.3 Pricing Strategy
```
My product is [product description]. My target customer is [customer type] with a budget of roughly [budget range]. Give me 3 pricing options: (1) a penetration price to maximize early sales, (2) a value-based price that reflects the outcome I deliver, (3) a premium price with what I'd need to add to justify it. For each, estimate conversion rate and monthly revenue at 1,000 visitors/month.
```

### 1.4 One-Page Business Model
```
Write me a one-page business model for [product/service]. Include: value proposition (one sentence), customer segments, revenue streams, cost structure, key metrics to track, and the one thing that kills this business if I get it wrong. Format as a clean document I can reference weekly.
```

### 1.5 90-Day Launch Plan
```
I have [available hours per week] hours/week and $[budget] to spend. I'm building [product]. Create a 90-day launch plan broken into 3 phases: Build (weeks 1-4), Launch (weeks 5-8), Grow (weeks 9-12). For each week, give me exactly 3 priorities — no more. I need focus, not a wishlist.
```

### 1.6 Revenue Model Comparison
```
I'm considering these revenue models for [product]: (1) one-time purchase at $[price], (2) monthly subscription at $[price]/mo, (3) freemium with premium tier at $[price]/mo, (4) usage-based pricing. For each model, tell me: expected LTV, churn implications, cash flow pattern, and which one a bootstrapped solo founder should pick and why.
```

### 1.7 Customer Interview Script
```
I need to validate [product idea] by talking to [target audience]. Write me a 15-minute customer interview script with 8 open-ended questions. Rules: no leading questions, no pitching the product, focus on understanding their current pain and what they've tried. Include follow-up probes for each question.
```

### 1.8 Pivot Decision Framework
```
My product [product name] has been live for [time period]. Current metrics: [key metrics — MRR, users, churn, etc.]. I'm considering pivoting to [new direction]. Give me a framework to decide: what signals say pivot, what signals say stay the course, and what's the minimum viable test I can run in 2 weeks to get a clear answer.
```

### 1.9 Unit Economics Calculator
```
Help me calculate unit economics for [product]. Inputs: price is $[price], estimated CAC is $[CAC], estimated monthly churn is [X]%, gross margin is [X]%, average support cost per customer is $[X]/month. Calculate: LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period, and break-even customer count. Tell me if this business is viable and what lever moves the needle most.
```

### 1.10 Moat Assessment
```
My product is [product description]. I'm a solo founder with no VC, no team, and limited time. What realistic moats can I build? Rank these options for my situation: (1) network effects, (2) switching costs, (3) brand/community, (4) proprietary data, (5) speed of execution. For each, give me one concrete action I can take this week.
```

## 2. Marketing Copy (10 prompts)

### 2.1 Landing Page Hero Section
```
Write a landing page hero section for [product]. Target audience: [audience]. The main transformation: [before state] → [after state]. Include: headline (under 10 words), subheadline (one sentence), 3 bullet points, and a CTA button. Tone: [confident/casual/technical/urgent]. No fluff. Every word earns its place.
```

### 2.2 Email Welcome Sequence
```
Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers to [product/newsletter]. Emails spaced 1, 3, 5, 7, 10 days apart. Email 1: deliver the promised value. Email 2: share a quick win. Email 3: tell a story about why I built this. Email 4: address the #1 objection. Email 5: make the offer. Keep each email under 200 words. Subject lines included.
```

### 2.3 Product Description (Marketplace)
```
Write a product description for [product] to sell on [Gumroad/Etsy/Amazon]. Price: $[price]. Include: hook (first line must stop the scroll), what's included (specific, not vague), who it's for (3 types of buyer), what makes it different (vs. alternatives), and a scarcity/urgency element. Max 300 words.
```

### 2.4 Cold DM Template
```
I want to reach [target person type — e.g., agency owners, newsletter creators] to tell them about [product/offer]. Write 3 cold DM templates for [Twitter/LinkedIn/Instagram]. Rules: no "Hey! I noticed..." openers, lead with value, under 60 words each, include a soft CTA. One should be direct, one curiosity-based, one social-proof-based.
```

### 2.5 Ad Copy Variations
```
Write 5 variations of ad copy for [product] targeting [audience] on [platform: Twitter/Facebook/Reddit]. Each variation should use a different angle: (1) pain point, (2) social proof, (3) curiosity gap, (4) transformation, (5) contrarian take. Max 80 words each. Include a CTA. No emojis unless I ask.
```

### 2.6 Testimonial Request
```
I need to ask early customers of [product] for testimonials. Write 3 outreach messages: (1) for happy customers who mentioned they liked it, (2) for silent customers who haven't said anything, (3) for customers who had a support issue that got resolved. Each message should make it easy to respond — include 3 specific prompting questions they can answer.
```

### 2.7 Product Launch Announcement
```
Write a launch announcement for [product] that I can post on [Twitter/LinkedIn/Reddit/Product Hunt]. Include: what it is (one line), why I built it, what's inside, who it's for, and the link. Tone: [excited but not cringe / technical and direct / casual and confident]. Under 250 words. End with a question to drive comments.
```

### 2.8 Comparison Page Copy
```
Write comparison copy for my product [product] vs. [competitor 1] and [competitor 2]. Format as a feature comparison table plus 3 paragraphs explaining why someone should choose mine. Be honest about where competitors are stronger — it builds trust. Focus on the specific use case where my product wins clearly.
```

### 2.9 Objection-Handling FAQ
```
My product is [product] at $[price]. My target buyer is [buyer type]. Write a 10-question FAQ that handles the most common objections: price, trust, competitors, technical requirements, refund policy, support, time to results, and "do I really need this?" Each answer should be 2-3 sentences max. Direct, no corporate speak.
```

### 2.10 Abandoned Cart / Follow-Up Email
```
Write a 3-email abandoned cart sequence for someone who visited [product page] but didn't buy. Email 1 (1 hour later): remind + remove friction. Email 2 (24 hours later): address the likely objection. Email 3 (72 hours later): create urgency with a time-limited bonus or discount. Subject lines included. Under 100 words each.
```

## 3. Content Creation (10 prompts)

### 3.1 Twitter Thread
```
Write a Twitter thread (7-10 tweets) about [topic relevant to your product/niche]. The thread should: hook with a surprising stat or contrarian take in tweet 1, deliver actionable value in tweets 2-8, soft-sell my product in the last tweet. No "thread" emoji. No "1/" numbering. Each tweet stands alone if someone screenshots it.
```

### 3.2 Blog Post Outline
```
Write a 1,500-word blog post outline for: "[title targeting keyword: {keyword}]". Structure: intro with hook (why should I care?), 5-7 H2 sections with 2-3 key points each, a section naturally mentioning my product [product] as a solution, conclusion with CTA. Include SEO meta title (under 60 chars) and meta description (under 155 chars).
```

### 3.3 Newsletter Issue
```
Write a newsletter issue for my [niche] audience. Subject line options (give me 3). Format: one main insight (3 paragraphs), one quick tip, one tool/resource recommendation, one CTA to [product/content]. Total length: 400-500 words. Tone: like a smart friend sending a useful email, not a brand broadcasting.
```

### 3.4 YouTube Video Script
```
Write a YouTube script for a [X]-minute video titled "[title]". Structure: hook (first 15 seconds — pattern interrupt), intro (who I am, why listen, 30 sec), main content (3-5 sections with transitions), CTA (subscribe + product mention). Include B-roll suggestions in [brackets]. Written for someone talking to camera, not reading an essay.
```

### 3.5 LinkedIn Post
```
Write a LinkedIn post about [topic] that positions me as a [role/expert type]. Format: bold opening line, short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each), a story or specific example, a takeaway the reader can use today, and a question at the end. No hashtag spam — max 3 relevant hashtags. Under 300 words.
```

### 3.6 TikTok/Reels Script
```
Write a 30-second TikTok script about [topic]. Structure: Hook (0-3 sec, pattern interrupt — what stops the scroll), Setup (3-10 sec, context), Payoff (10-25 sec, the value), CTA (25-30 sec, follow/comment/link). Write it as spoken word, not text. Include on-screen text suggestions in [brackets].
```

### 3.7 Case Study Template
```
Turn this customer result into a case study: [customer name/type] used [product] and achieved [result]. Structure: The Challenge (what were they struggling with?), The Solution (how did they use the product?), The Result (specific numbers), Key Takeaway (what can others learn?). Under 500 words. Include a pull quote.
```

### 3.8 Reddit Value Post
```
Write a Reddit post for r/[subreddit] about [topic related to my product]. Rules: NO self-promotion in the first 80% of the post. Lead with genuine value, personal experience, or a breakdown that helps the reader. Mention my product only once, at the end, as "I built something for this" with the link. Match the subreddit's tone.
```

### 3.9 Content Calendar (1 Week)
```
Create a 7-day content calendar for [platform: Twitter/LinkedIn/TikTok]. My niche is [niche]. Post frequency: [X per day]. For each day, give me: content type (thread/tip/story/question/meme), topic, a one-line hook, and estimated engagement level (high/medium/low). Mix value posts, engagement bait, and soft sells at a 70/20/10 ratio.
```

### 3.10 SEO Keyword Cluster
```
I'm building content for [product/niche]. Give me a keyword cluster: 1 pillar keyword (high volume), 5 supporting keywords (medium volume, more specific), and 10 long-tail keywords (low competition, high intent). For each keyword, suggest a content type (blog post, landing page, comparison page, tool page) and a working title. Prioritize keywords where a solo founder can actually rank.
```

## 4. Product Development (10 prompts)

### 4.1 MVP Scope Definition
```
I want to build [product idea]. I have [X weeks] and I'm building solo. Define the absolute minimum viable product: list the 5 must-have features and the 10 features I should NOT build yet (even though I'll want to). For each must-have, give me the simplest possible implementation. The goal: something I can ship and charge for in [X weeks].
```

### 4.2 User Story Set
```
Write 10 user stories for [product]. Format: "As a [user type], I want to [action] so that [outcome]." Include acceptance criteria for each (2-3 bullet points defining "done"). Prioritize by: P0 (blocks launch), P1 (first week after launch), P2 (month 1). Be specific — "view dashboard" is not a user story.
```

### 4.3 Technical Architecture Decision
```
I'm building [product] as a solo developer. I need to choose between: [Option A] and [Option B] for [component — e.g., database, hosting, framework]. My constraints: [budget], [timeline], [skill level]. Compare them on: learning curve, cost at 0/100/1000 users, maintenance burden, and vendor lock-in. Recommend one and tell me why. I can't afford to pick wrong and rebuild.
```

### 4.4 Launch Checklist
```
Create a pre-launch checklist for [product type: SaaS/digital product/mobile app]. Cover: technical (monitoring, backups, error handling), marketing (landing page, email list, social), legal (terms, privacy, refund policy), and operations (support channel, onboarding flow, metrics tracking). Only include items that matter for a solo founder launching to the first 100 customers. Skip enterprise stuff.
```

### 4.5 Feature Prioritization
```
Here are 15 feature requests for [product]: [list features]. My constraints: solo dev, [X hours/week], need to keep current customers happy while attracting new ones. Prioritize using ICE scoring (Impact, Confidence, Ease — each 1-10). Give me the ranked list and tell me which 3 to build this month and which to kill entirely.
```

### 4.6 Onboarding Flow
```
Design an onboarding flow for new users of [product]. The user just signed up / purchased. Walk me through: what they see first, the 3 steps to their first "aha moment," where most users will drop off and how to prevent it, and what email to send 24 hours later. Keep it frictionless. Every extra step loses 20% of users.
```

### 4.7 Bug Report Template
```
Create a bug report template for [product] that I can give to early users. Include fields for: what happened, what they expected, steps to reproduce, device/browser, and screenshot upload. Make it dead simple — if it takes more than 2 minutes to fill out, nobody will use it. Format as a [Google Form/Notion template/GitHub issue template].
```

### 4.8 API Design Review
```
I'm designing an API for [product feature]. Here are my endpoints: [list endpoints with methods]. Review this design for: RESTful conventions, naming consistency, error handling approach, pagination strategy, authentication, and rate limiting. Flag anything that will cause problems at scale or be confusing for third-party developers. Suggest fixes.
```

### 4.9 Database Schema Review
```
Here's my database schema for [product]: [paste schema or describe tables/fields]. Review for: normalization issues, missing indexes, fields I'll definitely need later but forgot, potential performance bottlenecks at 10K/100K/1M rows, and any security concerns (PII storage, etc.). I'm using [database type]. Give me the revised schema.
```

### 4.10 Post-Launch Iteration Plan
```
My product [product] launched [X days/weeks] ago. Current metrics: [users, revenue, churn, NPS, top complaints]. Based on these numbers, what should I focus on for the next 30 days? Give me 3 categories: (1) fix — things that are broken or losing customers, (2) improve — things that work but could be better, (3) build — new features worth adding. Max 2 items per category.
```

## 5. Operations (10 prompts)

### 5.1 Standard Operating Procedure
```
Write an SOP for [process — e.g., onboarding a new customer, handling a refund, publishing a blog post, deploying code]. Format: trigger (what initiates this process), steps (numbered, specific, no ambiguity), tools used, time estimate, and common mistakes to avoid. This should be clear enough that someone with zero context could follow it.
```

### 5.2 Customer Support Playbook
```
Create a customer support playbook for [product]. Include response templates for: (1) pre-sale questions, (2) setup/installation issues, (3) feature requests, (4) refund requests, (5) bugs, (6) angry customers. Each template: under 100 words, professional but human, solves or escalates within one reply. I handle support solo — speed matters.
```

### 5.3 Hiring Brief (First Contractor)
```
I'm hiring my first contractor to help with [task type — e.g., content writing, customer support, development]. Write a job brief that includes: what they'll do (specific deliverables, not vague responsibilities), required skills, how I'll evaluate their work, pay range for [X hours/week], and 3 screening questions I should ask. I've never hired before — what mistakes should I avoid?
```

### 5.4 Weekly Review Template
```
Create a weekly review template for a solo founder running [product]. Sections: (1) key metrics this week vs. last week, (2) what shipped, (3) what didn't ship and why, (4) top customer feedback, (5) revenue and expenses, (6) biggest risk right now, (7) top 3 priorities for next week. Keep it to one page. I'll fill this out every Friday in 15 minutes.
```

### 5.5 Automation Audit
```
Here's how I currently spend my time running [product]: [describe daily/weekly tasks and time spent]. Identify the top 5 tasks I should automate, the tool to use for each (Zapier, Make, n8n, or a script), estimated setup time, and estimated hours saved per week. Only recommend automations with a payback period under 2 weeks. I don't want to spend a week automating something I do for 10 minutes.
```

### 5.6 Incident Response Plan
```
Write an incident response plan for [product — SaaS/API/e-commerce site]. Cover: (1) how to detect an outage (monitoring), (2) immediate response steps (first 15 minutes), (3) communication template for affected users, (4) root cause analysis template, (5) post-incident review process. I'm a solo operator — this needs to work when I'm woken up at 3am. Keep it actionable, not bureaucratic.
```

### 5.7 Financial Dashboard
```
Design a monthly financial dashboard for my solo business [product]. I need to track: revenue (by product/channel), expenses (by category), profit margin, MRR/ARR, CAC, LTV, runway, and month-over-month growth. Tell me which tool to build this in (Google Sheets/Notion/other), give me the exact columns and formulas, and tell me which 3 numbers matter most at my stage ([revenue level]).
```

### 5.8 Content Repurposing Workflow
```
I just created [content type — e.g., a blog post, YouTube video, Twitter thread] about [topic]. Give me a repurposing workflow that turns this one piece into 8-10 pieces of content across [platforms]. For each piece: format, platform, what to extract from the original, any modifications needed, and estimated time to create. I need a system, not a one-off.
```

### 5.9 Churn Reduction Playbook
```
My product [product] has a monthly churn rate of [X]%. My price is $[X]/mo. Analyze potential churn causes and give me a 5-step churn reduction playbook: (1) identify at-risk users (what signals to watch), (2) intervention email sequence (3 emails), (3) exit survey (5 questions), (4) win-back offer, (5) product changes that address the top 3 churn reasons. Each step should be implementable this week.
```

### 5.10 Legal Essentials Checklist
```
I'm launching [product type] as a solo founder. Give me a legal essentials checklist: terms of service (key clauses I need), privacy policy (especially for [data types I collect]), refund policy, intellectual property considerations, and business structure recommendation for [country]. I can't afford a lawyer yet — what can I handle with templates, and what absolutely requires professional help?
```
